Pre-requisites
  1. Problem Formulation

    2. They start by defining the problem they want to solve, such as object detection, image classification, or semantic segmentation.

  2. Dataset Selection

    3. Researchers choose appropriate datasets that are relevant to their problem domain. Common datasets include COCO, ImageNet, Pascal VOC, and Cityscapes.

  3. Model Architecture Selection

    4. Depending on the problem, researchers select or design a suitable neural network architecture. This could be a convolutional neural network (CNN), a recurrent neural network (RNN), or a combination of both.

  4. Training

    5. Researchers train their chosen model on the selected dataset using appropriate optimization algorithms and techniques, such as stochastic gradient descent (SGD), Adam, or RMSprop. They often fine-tune hyperparameters through experimentation.

  5. Validation

    6. Once trained, the model is evaluated on a separate validation set to assess its performance. This step helps to identify overfitting and tune model parameters further.

  6. Testing

    7. The final step involves testing the model on a held-out test set to evaluate its generalization performance. This step ensures that the model performs well on unseen data.

  7. Analysis and Interpretation

    8. Researchers analyze the results, interpret the findings, and compare them with state-of-the-art methods. They may also provide insights into the strengths and limitations of their approach.

  8. Documentation and Publication

    9. Finally, researchers document their experiments, results, and conclusions in a paper format suitable for submission to academic conferences or journals. This includes writing the introduction, related work, methodology, results, discussion, and conclusion sections.

Throughout this process, researchers often iterate on their experiments, refining their approach based on feedback from peers, reviewers, and their own insights.
